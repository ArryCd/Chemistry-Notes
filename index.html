Werner's theory ‚ÄĒ primary vs secondary valencycore concept▼
Three types of bonding
  • Ionic ‚ÄĒ electrostatic interaction between anion & cation; complete separation of electrons; complete transfer
  • Covalent ‚ÄĒ not a transfer; 2 electrons shared, 1 by each atom
  • Coordinate ‚ÄĒ one atom donates, not completely; donation of lone pair
K[Au(CN)‚āā] ‚ÄĒ worked example

Potassium aurocyanate K[Au(CN)‚āā] is used in gold-plating as an electrolyte. Here Au ‚Üí +1 (primary valency). This is a coordination compound where CN‚ĀĽ are ligands sharing electrons with Au‚Āļ.

Werner's geometric proof ‚ÄĒ why octahedral?PM Sirimportant▼

For MA‚āĄB‚āā type compounds, Werner tried to find how many isomers of [Co(NH‚āÉ)‚āĄCl‚āā] exist. He considered three possible geometries:

GeometryPredicted isomers for MA‚āĄB‚āāResult
Planar (metal in centre)3 arrangements possibleToo many
Trigonal prism3 arrangementsToo many
OctahedralOnly 2 (cis and trans)✓ Matches experiment

Werner was able to isolate only 2 isomers chemically, so he concluded the structure must be octahedral. This was the decisive experimental proof.

Mohr salt, alum, and similar compounds (non-coordination compounds) can take many forms. But coordination compounds with only 2 isomers ‚Üí octahedral structure is the only explanation.
Second compound type: MA‚āÉB‚āÉ

For [Co(NH‚āÉ)‚āÉCl‚āÉ] type: octahedral gives exactly 2 isomers (fac and mer). This further confirmed the octahedral geometry.

Why coordination compounds form ‚ÄĒ key insight

In a free Co¬≤‚Āļ ion, all 5 d-orbitals are degenerate (same energy). All 7 electrons have same energy ‚ÄĒ only possible if no ligands have come. When a ligand approaches along an axis, it repels electrons in orbitals along that axis more than others ‚Üí d-orbital splitting ‚Üí basis of CFT.

PM Sir's insight: the 4th electron in t‚āāg in a complex needs energy to either pair (pairing energy favorable) or go to the higher eg state. Which is cheaper decides high spin vs low spin. This is the decision point.
d-orbital shapes and the basis of CFTPM Sirfoundation▼

The 5 d-orbitals have distinct spatial orientations. In a free ion (no ligands), all 5 are degenerate. When ligands approach, orbitals pointing directly at ligands experience more repulsion.

Octahedral splitting ‚ÄĒ energy levels, őĒ‚āí and 10Dqcore▼
Octahedral field splitting: e‚āė (dz¬≤, dx¬≤-y¬≤) ‚Üí +0.6őĒ‚āí = +6Dq (higher energy) t‚āāg (dxy, dyz, dxz) ‚Üí ‚ąí0.4őĒ‚āí = ‚ąí4Dq (lower energy) Total gap = őĒ‚āí = 10Dq Note: Dq is an older notation, 10Dq = őĒ‚āí = the energy gap
Tetrahedral splitting ‚ÄĒ inverted
Tetrahedral field (ligands approach between axes, not along them): t‚āā (dxy, dyz, dxz) ‚Üí higher energy (+4Dq_tet) e (dz¬≤, dx¬≤-y¬≤) ‚Üí lower energy (‚ąí6Dq_tet) Key relationship: (10Dq)tet = (4/9)(10Dq)oct = őĒt = (4/9)őĒ‚āí
Square planar splitting ‚ÄĒ from PM Sir notes
Splitting order (bottom to top): dz¬≤ (lowest) dyz, dxz dxy dx¬≤-y¬≤ (highest ‚ÄĒ points directly at 4 ligands in xy plane)
Square planar has a much larger energy gap for dx¬≤-y¬≤ than octahedral, because removing the two z-axis ligands pushes the xy-plane ligands closer in ‚Üí dx¬≤-y¬≤ shoots up in energy.
Factors affecting őĒ‚āí (CFSE magnitude)PM Sirimportant▼
1. Strength of ligand (spectrochemical series)
Weak field: Cl‚ĀĽ, F‚ĀĽ, Br‚ĀĽ, I‚ĀĽ, water ‚Üí small őĒ‚āí ‚Üí high spin Moderate field: NH‚āÉ ‚Üí intermediate Strong field: CO, CN‚ĀĽ ‚Üí large őĒ‚āí ‚Üí low spin
2. Charge of metal ion/cation

Higher charge ‚Üí metal pulls ligands closer ‚Üí stronger ligand field ‚Üí larger őĒ‚āí. Example from PM Sir notes: [V(H‚āāO)‚āÜ]¬≥‚Āļ > [V(H‚āāO)‚āÜ]¬≤‚Āļ in CFSE because 3+ pulls water ligands closer than 2+.

3. Nature of metal ion ‚ÄĒ period effect
Splitting increases going down a group (same charge): 3d < 4d < 5d Example: Co¬≥‚Āļ < Rh¬≥‚Āļ < Ir¬≥‚Āļ Heavier metals have more diffuse d-orbitals ‚Üí stronger interaction with ligands

High spin vs low spin ‚ÄĒ the 4th electron decisionPM Sirkey concept▼

For d‚Āī and above, the 4th electron has a choice: pair in t‚āāg (costs pairing energy P) or go to eg (costs őĒ‚āí). Which is cheaper determines spin state.

If őĒ‚āí > P ‚Üí pairing is cheaper ‚Üí LOW SPIN (strong field) If őĒ‚āí < P ‚Üí going to eg is cheaper ‚Üí HIGH SPIN (weak field) For tetrahedral: őĒt = (4/9)őĒ‚āí, so őĒt << P almost always ‚Üí tetrahedral complexes are almost always high spin
This is why tetrahedral complexes are rarely low spin ‚ÄĒ the splitting is just too small to overcome pairing energy.
High spin configurations (used often)
d‚ĀĶ high spin: t‚āāg¬≥ eg¬≤ (all singly occupied ‚ÄĒ 5 unpaired) d‚Ā∂ high spin: t‚āāg‚Āī eg¬≤ (4 unpaired) d‚Ā∑ high spin: t‚āāg‚ĀĶ eg¬≤ (3 unpaired)
CFSE formula and general methodmethod▼
CFSE = (n_t‚āāg √ó ‚ąí4Dq) + (n_eg √ó +6Dq) + extra pairing energy Or in őĒ‚āí units: CFSE = (n_t‚āāg √ó ‚ąí0.4őĒ‚āí) + (n_eg √ó +0.6őĒ‚āí) + őĒP Where őĒP = (extra forced pairs vs free ion) √ó P
Only add pairing energy for pairs that are EXTRA compared to the free ion. If the free ion already has 2 pairs (like d‚Ā∂ high spin ‚Üí 1 pair; d‚Ā∂ low spin ‚Üí 3 pairs ‚Üí 2 extra pairs), then őĒP = 2P.

Spinel structure AB‚āāO‚āĄ ‚ÄĒ crystal structure basicsPM Sirimportant▼

Spinel formula: AB‚āāO‚āĄ, where A is A¬≤‚Āļ cation and B is B¬≥‚Āļ cation. O¬≤‚ĀĽ is a weak-field ligand ‚Üí all complexes are high spin.

Crystal structure

  • For each unit cell (containing 4 O¬≤‚ĀĽ ions): 8 tetrahedral holes and 4 octahedral holes available
  • eg level ‚Üí e‚ĀĽ repulsion (size increases with eg filling)
  • t‚āāg level ‚Üí no e‚ĀĽ repulsion (orbitals point away from ligands)

Ionic radius trend across 3d series ‚ÄĒ CFT applicationPM Sirapplication▼

PM Sir drew the ionic radius vs 3d metal ion graph (aqua complexes). The curve is a double-humped shape, not a smooth decrease. This is directly due to CFT:

  • Filling t‚āāg orbitals (which point away from ligands) ‚Üí does NOT cause extra repulsion ‚Üí metal-ligand bond can shorten ‚Üí radius decreases
  • Filling eg orbitals (which point at ligands) ‚Üí causes direct repulsion ‚Üí pushes ligands away ‚Üí effective radius increases at Ca and Zn-like positions
  • The double hump appears at d‚ĀĶ (Mn¬≤‚Āļ) where both sets are half-filled symmetrically, and at d¬Ļ‚Āį (Zn¬≤‚Āļ) where no CFSE exists
3d series radii trend for M¬≤‚Āļ aqua complexes: Ti¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí V¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí Cr¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí Mn¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí Fe¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí Co¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí Ni¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí Cu¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí Zn¬≤‚Āļ (double-humped decrease, not smooth) eg filling ‚Üí e‚ĀĽ repulsion ‚Üí size increases t‚āāg filling ‚Üí no e‚ĀĽ repulsion ‚Üí size decreases (CFSE stabilisation)

Thermogravimetric analysis ‚ÄĒ CuSO‚āĄ¬∑5H‚āāOTKM Sirnew▼

Thermogravimetric method tracks mass loss as temperature increases. For CuSO‚āĄ¬∑5H‚āāO, water molecules leave in stages at different temperatures.

TG curve for CuSO‚āĄ¬∑5H‚āāO (weight vs temperature): Step 1: ‚ąí18 (weight loss) ‚Üí 2 H‚āāO molecules escape Step 2: ‚ąí18 (weight loss) ‚Üí 2 more H‚āāO molecules escape Step 3: ‚ąí54 ‚Üí last H‚āāO + further dehydration products Each plateau on the curve = a stable intermediate compound The curve has 3 distinct weight-loss steps ‚Üí confirms 5 water molecules in 3 different environments
The thermogravimetric method tells you not just how many water molecules are present, but in how many distinct environments ‚ÄĒ some waters are more tightly bound than others.
